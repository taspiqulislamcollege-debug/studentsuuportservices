Financial Support

Scholarship Support

Three ways to get more money for college — start with Step 1 every semester before moving to external scholarships.

Step 1
📩

Appeal Your Financial Aid

Every semester, appeal your financial aid through St. John's University. You may get a rejection letter saying the school doesn't have enough funding — keep trying every semester anyway. It costs nothing and you could receive additional funding.

📩 Appeal Financial Aid →
Step 2
🏛️

Apply to SJU Endowment Scholarships

Complete the general application for endowment scholarships offered directly by St. John's University. These are scholarships funded by donors specifically for SJU students — apply every year.

🏛️ Apply on AcademicWorks →
Step 3
🌐

Apply to External Scholarships

Search and apply to outside scholarships from organizations, companies, and foundations. Browse our curated list — filtered by your major, eligibility, and upcoming deadlines.

🔍 Browse Scholarship Finder →

Tuition Support Grants

SSS Supplemental Grant Funding

📅 When to Request

Grants may be requested after your first semester as an active SSS student. Requests must be submitted during the semester prior to the one for which funding is sought (e.g., request a spring grant during the fall semester).

✅ Eligibility Requirements

To be eligible, students must be active SSS participants. This includes:

  • Attending at least 3 events or workshops each semester
  • Communicating with SSS staff if unable to attend
  • Responding to required emails
  • Reviewing program resources
  • Participating in check-ins and advising sessions

ℹ️ Important Notes

Grants are not guaranteed and are awarded based on available funding, student engagement, and demonstrated need. Students must be Pell Grant recipients and have an outstanding balance on their account at the time Financial Services applies the grant.
